U9 Academy

WHO IS THE ACADEMY FOR?

The Academy is a development program is for boys and girls ages 7, 8 and 9 years old, who have a desire to develop their soccer skills in a fun environment with other players who share their passion for the game, and are looking for a challenge beyond recreational soccer. 

Younger U8 players will be considered if their abilities are appropriate. 

2013 Season 7 and 8 year olds (U9) age group birth dates: August 1 2004 to July 31st 2006.

THE ACADEMY GOALS AND FOCUS

Our program will focus on technical (skill-based) training and free play.  The emphasis will be on skill development, where kids learn through direct experience and hands-on play; players will learn best “by doing.”  Technical training, tactical decision-making, and positional play are just a few areas that will be taught by the highly knowledgeable staff.  Emphasis is always placed on teaching the basic rules, techniques, and skills that are essential to improve one's soccer game.  Elements emphasized include:  1 vs 1, passing, receiving, shielding, decision-making skills and ball mastery.  But the key to all of this is to have fun! 

Academy Team Players and Development Players

The Academy Program will feature two levels of play and commitment:

Academy Team Players are selected at tryouts and will participate in two trainings per week, plus play dates or a tournament three to four weekends in the Spring.  They will participate in the NorCal Academy League and Tournaments in the Fall.

Development Players may, but are not required to come to tryouts.  They will practice with the Academy Teams one day a week, while continuing to play for their Dixie, San Rafael or Ross Valley Youth Soccer Clubs.  

There will be Spring and Fall Programs for both levels.  The Feb./March Tryouts are for both Spring and Fall.

How Much Does the Program Cost?

Academy Team Players:       

Spring program will be approx. $350 for 8 weeks.

Fall program is approx. $650 for 15-17 week session (Late July to Mid November)

Development players:           

The cost for the Spring trainings is $200. (1 per week for 8 weeks)

The Cost for the Fall is anticipated to be $300 (for 10-12 weeks)

The Central Marin Soccer Club partially subsidizes the Academy Program so that families have the opportunity to transition from recreational soccer to the competitive program.   Partial Scholarships are also available.

How do I sign up?

The Academy Team Players will be selected to play for a team during the tryout process.  They will register through Central Marin Soccer and US Club and have player cards, which will be for both the Spring and Fall.  This will be done on the Club’s Registration Night after Tryouts.

The Development Players will register during the registration meeting, with Central Marin through US Soccer as a development recreation player.  They will be placed on a development player list and will pay the additional fee (above) by check or cash to Central Marin Soccer.  They will also register through their recreational club so they are placed on a Recreation team roster.

When are the Practices and Training Sessions?

There will be a Spring Session beginning approximately April 1 and continue thru May with (2) trainings per week and play dates every other weekend (Sunday).  Sessions will be in the Ross Area, Central Marin/San Rafael and Terra Linda/Dixie Fields.  Final Schedules will be provided as soon as fields can be retained.  Hall Middle School fields will be retained in case of rainy weather and for the (4) Sunday play dates.

How Many Games will they Play?

Fall Academy Team Players start training July 22rd week and play through mid November.  They will play 2-4 weekends of play dates and also play 8-10 league games between other local Club Academy teams.  They will play in 2 to 3 Tournaments, including our Premier Challenge Tournament: Mid August; and at least one other tournament in a near by community and possibly the Sacrament Boo Festival.  Developmental Players will play their games with their Recreation teams.
